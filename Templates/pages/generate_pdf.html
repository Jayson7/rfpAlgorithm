Plan:
Consider the use of acetylsalicylic acid (aspirin) during pregnancy. Generally, a dose of 75-100 mg is recommended starting from the first trimester, preferably before the 16th week of gestation, and continue its use throughout the pregnancy. If the patient weighs more than 90 kg, increasing the dose to 150 mg per day may be considered. • Follow-up: by your obstetrician and midwife. It is important to note that the final decision to initiate aspirin use, as well as determining the appropriate dosage, should be made by the obstetrician or specialist in charge of the patient. The doctor will evaluate the situation on an individual basis and consider the need for additional complementary tests before making a final decision.
